Washington State Stroke Designation Levels

Level I - Comprehensive Stroke Center

Highest level of stroke care. Provides complete range of services including 24/7 neurosurgical coverage, advanced imaging, endovascular procedures (thrombectomy), and comprehensive rehabilitation. Can treat the most complex stroke cases.

Level II - Intermediate Stroke Center

Provides acute stroke care with CT/MRI capabilities, IV thrombolysis (tPA), and stroke unit. May have telemedicine consultation with comprehensive centers. Can stabilize and treat most stroke patients, with transfer protocols for complex cases.

Level III - Primary Stroke Center

Basic acute stroke care with CT scanning, IV thrombolysis capability, and emergency protocols. Focuses on rapid assessment and treatment with established transfer agreements for patients requiring higher-level care.

Note: These WA State designations are separate from national stroke center certifications (Joint Commission, DNV). Many hospitals have both state designations and national certifications.
